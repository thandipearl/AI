# AI Financial & Market Data Analysis with Python


**What can data tell us about how big tech companies are investing in AI… and what they’re getting back from it?**


In this analysis, I worked with data from three major companies:
- Google  
- OpenAI  
- Meta  

Each of them is racing to lead in artificial intelligence.

---



## 1. Who is spending the most on innovation?

I looked at how much each company is investing in research and development.

**What I found:**
- Google is leading by a large margin  
- Meta follows behind  
- OpenAI is investing much less in comparison  

This already tells us about scale and resources.

---

### 2. Is that spending turning into revenue?

Next, I compared spending to the money these companies are making from AI.

**The pattern:**
- The more a company spends, the more it tends to earn  
- The gap between spending and revenue changes over time  

Over the years, revenue starts catching up with spending.

---

### 3. How do events affect stock performance?

I explored how major AI events impact stock prices.

This included:
- Model launches  
- Product updates  
- AI-related announcements  

**What stood out:**
- Big launches often lead to noticeable spikes  
- Some events caused drops, showing mixed reactions  

This is where the data started to feel alive.

---

### 4. How is growth changing over time?

I tracked AI revenue growth year by year.

**Trend:**
- Growth is increasing over time  
- Some companies are stable  
- Others have sharp spikes  

This shows how fast-moving the AI space is.

---

### 5. What relationships exist in the data?

I explored how different factors relate to each other.

**Key insight:**
- When research spending increases, revenue usually increases too  

However:
- Stock changes do not always follow the same pattern  

---

### 6. What happens over time?

Looking at the bigger picture:

- Spending and revenue both increase over time  
- Earlier years show higher spending than revenue  
- Over time, revenue starts catching up  

This suggests investments are starting to pay off.

---

### 7. Before vs After: Event Impact

One of the most interesting parts was focusing on specific events.

I looked at:
- Stock behavior before and after major events  

**Observation:**
- Major releases often cause sharp increases  
- You can clearly see the moment something big happened
